120 Years of Olympic History

Team Figma

In this project, we narrate some historically significant years as well as show how these events manifested in Olympic Data. We also walk you through how to use our Tableau dashboard to achieve the visualizations we use.

Our interactive Tableau dashboard is included for you to explore any years we may not have narrated!
Dataset Overview The Olympics dataset is sourced from Kaggle , spanning the games from Athens 1896 to Rio 2016.

Each time an athlete competes in the Olympics corresponds to one row of the dataset.
(regardless of medal status). There are 271,116 rows total.

The advent of the Olympics 1896 Hosted in Athens, Greece, the first Olympics are far removed from the modern day Olympics.

Fewer than 20 teams participated in 43 events.

Over 65% of the participants were Greek.

Medal composition Moving the "Select Year" slider all the way to the left at 1896, we can see the medal composition for the games in this year.

Unsurprisingly, Greece sweeps the podium.
Competitor Map With the 9 sports of this winter Games selected in the sport filter and the medal filter set to "All", we can see what countries were represented.

As expected, Germany had no athletes competing.
The first winter games 1924 In 1924, the first Olympic Winter games were hosted in Chamonix, France with nine distinct events.

Germany was banned from participating, following the events of World War 1.
"The Austerity Games"

1948

Dubbed “The Austerity Games”, the 1948 Olympics were hosted in old venues in London.  Germany and Japan were not invited to participate, following the events of World War 2.

Czechoslovakian athlete Marie Provazníková refused to return home, citing lack of freedom under the Soviet bloc.
Gold Medals Moving the year slider to 1948, and setting the "Filter by Medal" to "Gold", a user can see the choropleth map and bar chart of gold medals for this year.
The Boycott Shown in the plot below is a line chart of medals over time for the USA.

Due to their boycott of the 1980 Summer Games, there is a drastic dip on the chart.

It also shows that in response to USA's 1980 boycott, the Soviet Union and most of the Eastern Bloc boycotted the 1984 games. With their greatest competition absent, the USA experienced tremendous success in the 1984 games.
"Miracle on Ice"

1980

Summer
The United States boycotted the 1980 Moscow Olympics along with 64 other countries in protest of the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan.

The Soviet Union, along with East Germany, won more than half of all medals.

Winter (fun fact)
Dubbed “the miracle on ice”, the United States hockey team, composed of amateur and minor league players, toppled the heavily favored (and previously undefeated) Soviet team.
